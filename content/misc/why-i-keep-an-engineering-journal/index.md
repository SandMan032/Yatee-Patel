{{< lead >}}
You don't really understand a system until you've had to explain it to someone
who wasn't in your head when you built it.
{{< /lead >}}

Most of what I build lives in a terminal and disappears the moment I close the
laptop. This journal is an attempt to make some of it stick.

## Three reasons this exists

1. **Writing is debugging for your own understanding.** The moment I try to
   describe why a design works, the hand-wavy parts become obvious.
2. **Future-me is a stranger.** In six months I won't remember why I chose a
   bounded channel over a ring buffer. A paragraph now saves an afternoon later.
3. **Teaching compounds.** If one other person building something similar finds
   a post useful, the time spent writing it pays for itself.

> The palest ink is better than the best memory.

## What you'll find here

- **Projects** — the work grouped end-to-end, with the posts documenting each.
- **Learning Log** — notes on Go, Docker and systems design as I pick them up.
- **Misc** — shorter, less structured thoughts like this one.

None of it is polished on the first pass. That's the point — this is a working
notebook, published in the open.



If any of it is useful to you, that's a bonus. Mostly, I'm writing it for the
version of me who has to maintain all this next quarter.
